Our Story

A Century & A Half
of Faith

In 1876, the London Missionary Society arrived at the shores of Hula Village. Reverend Dr W.G. Lawes and his fellow missionaries braved uncharted waters to carry the message of the Gospel to Papua New Guinea's coastline — planting seeds of faith that have grown for 150 years.

"A legacy built on sacrifice, service, and the unyielding
hope of those who came before us."

Today, as we stand at the threshold of a remarkable sesquicentennial, we honour their memory — and look forward to a week-long celebration of faith, culture, and community.

In Memoriam

Honouring the Pioneers

Dr W.G. Lawes London Missionary Society · 1876

The Dr W.G. Lawes Memorial Stone

Vula'a Iruna

The Vula'a Iruna Memorial Stone stands at Hula Village as an enduring testament to the selfless mission of Dr W.G. Lawes and those who followed him. It marks a sacred place where the first seeds of the Christian faith were sown on Papua New Guinea's southern coast.

This Corporate Dinner is more than a fundraising event — it is a gathering of gratitude, remembrance, and hope, as we unite to celebrate 150 years of that transformative legacy.
